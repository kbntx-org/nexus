I'm a Senior Platform Engineer with a full-stack background. I grew into this role progressively. I joined PowerUs as a junior product engineer, gradually took ownership of DevOps and infrastructure topics on the side, and eventually it became my full-time focus.

<br>

What I enjoy most about platform work is the force multiplier effect. Building the tooling, pipelines, and systems that make an entire engineering team move faster and more confidently is genuinely satisfying to me, more so than shipping features for end users ever was.

<br>

Context matters to me. Understanding the product, the team's constraints, and the broader engineering challenges is what helps me build platform that actually makes sense — my product background keeps me grounded in what developers actually need day to day, rather than what's just technically impressive.

<br>

I also care about the developer experience of the platform itself, good abstractions, clear interfaces, and documentation that actually helps. Platform should feel like a product that engineers enjoy using, not a black box they're afraid to touch.
